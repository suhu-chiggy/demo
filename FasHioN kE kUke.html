CASUAL DAY WITH JACKETS

Casual winter day outfits can be super fun. It’s all about putting right pieces together and you are done to rock. You can go for denim or leather jackets to layer up. Wear right boots. And you are all set.                         

BRUNCH TIME IN CARDIGANS!

While for a brunch , you can choose something simple yet fashionable. For this, you can go for long woollen cardigans which are funky, stylish and bring the best in you. You can make the statement effortlessly by wearing them.

WINTER WORK OUTFITS

For work in winter, you can simply add blazers and jackets to your usual outfit. This addition of extra layer keeps you warm yet sophisticated. Simple black and white striped tee. Black jeans. Black leather jacket. Coolest work outfit!

EVENING DATES

Well, dates are a thing. No matter what point of day it is, we should consider dressing special.Pairing up dress with thigh high boots will be great for a date. You can try putting on cardigan of your choice You can also go for sweatshirts of different patterns that make you look out of the box.

PARTY NIGHTS

Partying in winter clothes isn’t so fun. you  go with super sexy bottoms and layer up at the top. Like, go for a skirt, boots and cute sweatshirt. Or a tank top layered with good jacket. You can also go for dresses, but with high boots to protect your legs from cold.

Hair & Skin Regime Pre and Post Holi

Wish you a very happy and colorful Holi my dear lovelies.

The most vibrant, colorful and joyful festival of the year is here – Holi. Holi is a festival of food, fun, and mingling together, so let’s keep the vibe live and not worry about our skin and hair to get damaged by following these simple steps. Be prepared to deal with the colors of your skin and hair. Harmful chemicals can strip the natural oils from your skin but don’t worry we have got you covered.

....pre holi care

.....post holi care

LEARN MORE LIVE DEMO
PRE HOLI CARE 
  • Cover your body -Try to wear full sleeve top and full-length bottoms, try to cover maximum parts of your body to prevent your skin from colors, sun, and chemicals.
  • Apply Waterproof Sunscreen - Apply Sunscreen on your face, at least ten minutes before stepping out of the house of minimum 35 SPF, to save your skin from the harsh sun.
  • Apply Nail Paint & Petroleum Jelly -You can remove colors from face and body but it’s very difficult to remove it from nails and cuticles so apply petroleum jelly on cuticles and put transparent or any color nail paint on your nails.
  • Apply oil -Apply coconut or any other regular hair oil half an hour in your hair before stepping out. Make sure you apply a good quantity and tie them, do not keep them open to avoid the damage caused by chemical colors.
  • Apply Lip Balm -Apply at least 2 layers of lip balm or lipstick to avoid colors to stick to your lips.
  • Apply Moisturizer -I would say, take bath in moisturizer because that’s the only way you can save your skin from colors. You can also go for olive or coconut oil instead of moisturizer, make sure of applying good quantity, double of what you do on an everyday basis. Most importantly, do not forget to moisturize your ears, neck, and back as it can get really difficult to remove color from there.
POST HOLI CARE
  • Use Homemade Cleanser -Make your own cleanser – take a bowl of curd and put two tablespoons of lemon juice in it. Apply it to the whole body and face, wait for few minutes then wash it off with normal or lukewarm water.
  • Homemade face packs -You can apply besan and milk face pack on your face. Let is sit on your face for some time, once it dries out, gently massage it and then wash it with regular or lukewarm water. Don’t forget to apply moisturizer post that.
  • Color Stains on teeth -If you get color stains on teeth, then mix some salt and lemon drops in your toothpaste and brush your teeth mildly.
  • Hair Mask -Wash your hair with a mild or baby shampoo to remove oil, dirt, and color, followed by conditioner. 
  • No hot water -Post Holi, do not use hot water for washing hair or for a bath as it can make your skin and hair really dry. If it’s really cold outside then you can use lukewarm water.
  • No soaps -Avoid using face washes and soaps as they are alkaline in nature which will make your skin even drier.
